About us

More than a TV show — a movement

Founded in 2008, Sesame Square Nigeria Foundation is a fully incorporated Nigerian entity and the official West African arm of Sesame Workshop. What began as a USAID and PEPFAR project has grown into 15+ years of educational impact across five countries.

Our story
Nigeria's first Sesame Street co-production

Sesame Square launched on NTA in 2011 — West Africa's first Sesame Street adaptation. Produced by a multidisciplinary Nigerian team with technical support from Sesame Workshop's New York office, it features beloved Nigerian Muppets Kami, Zobi, and Raya, alongside Big Bird and Elmo revoiced with Nigerian accents.

Our reach
From Abuja to Accra and beyond

Our Nigerian team has developed educational projects for children, families, and educators across Nigeria, Ghana, Niger, Mali, and Senegal. Through literacy kits, teacher trainings, community screenings, and the Hausa adaptation Dandalin Sesame, we reach the most underserved communities in West Africa.

Meet the Muppets

Sesame Square's beloved characters

Our Nigerian Muppets — created by Sesame Workshop — bring education, warmth, and Nigerian culture to life for millions of children.

Kami
Sesame Square · Nigeria

A warm, curious yellow Muppet who teaches children about acceptance, health, and the joy of learning. Wise, kind, and full of life.

Zobi
Yam Monster · Nigeria

Nigeria's loveable blue Muppet — passionate about yams and just as passionate about learning. Zobi brings humour and heart to every lesson.

Raya
Sesame Square · Nigeria

A spirited champion for girls' empowerment. Raya inspires children — especially girls — to dream big, stay in school, and believe in themselves.

Big Bird
Sesame Street · Global

The iconic six-year-old bird from Sesame Street — revoiced with a Nigerian accent for Sesame Square, bringing warmth and curiosity to Nigerian children.

Our work

What we do

From television to teacher training, we deploy research-backed Sesame Workshop programmes adapted specifically for Nigerian and West African children.

Media
Educational television

Sesame Square airs on NTA, reaching millions of children aged 3–7 with curriculum covering literacy, numeracy, health, and Nigerian cultural pride.

Literacy
Learning kits & materials

English and Hausa literacy kits — alphabet cards, storybooks, speaking books, and puppet kits — distributed to schools and communities nationwide.

Health
Health & hygiene education

The WASH UP! and "Cleaner, Healthier, Happier" initiatives — backed by the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation — promote sanitation in children and caregivers.

Capacity
Teacher training

Working through SUBEB and state boards, we train master educators to deliver child-friendly, inclusive learning environments across Nigeria.

Technology
Mobile learning

Via the Worldreader app, Sesame Square content reaches 30+ mobile stories to families without TV access across Nigeria.

Regional
West Africa programmes

Our Nigerian team leads educational projects in Ghana, Niger, Mali, and Senegal — with full technical support from Sesame Workshop New York.

Our global partner
Powered by Sesame Workshop

Every programme we run is built on Sesame Workshop's 50+ years of research — the proven framework behind Sesame Street, reaching 150 million children in 190+ countries. Sesame Square is Sesame Workshop's official West African partner.
